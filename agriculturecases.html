Subtask 2 - Resource Allocation

SDG Goal: 11

Target: 11.4

Indicator: 11.4.1

In the context of our city Shymora, which is a valley followed by mountain terrains where terrace farming is being done. Terrace farming requires efficient resource allocation. If you have a limited supply of water or fertilizer, it’s important to prioritize the most critical terraces. To solve this, we will assign a priority for each terrace based on factors like water usage, crop growth stage, or soil fertility. Then, allocate the available resources starting with the highest priority. To implement this, we will use a max heap or min-heap to select which terrace should receive resources first based on priority.

Subtask 3 - Sorting Terrace Zones by Soil Quality

SDG Goal: 11

Target: 11.7

Indicator: 11.7.1

In terrace farming, there is uneven distribution of cultivable and fertile land. Therefore, we need to sort the terraces based on soil quality, so we can prioritize which terraces need more attention. Terrace farming often involves monitoring the soil quality of different areas. Sorting these terraces allows farmers to prioritize resources where they’re needed most. To implement this, we will use the quick sort algorithm.
